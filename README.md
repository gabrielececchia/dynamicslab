# The DynamicsLab

The DynamicsLab is a personal project exploring vehicle dynamics through code — a space where theory meets simulation. It collects models, tools, and analyses built in Python and MATLAB, covering topics like tire behavior, suspension kinematics, load transfer, and lap time simulation.
The project grew out of my work as a vehicle dynamics engineer (and now technical lead) in Formula Student, where I went from chassis design to owning vehicle dynamics for the team. What started as internal tools for race car setup and analysis evolved into something I wanted to share more broadly — breaking down the math and physics behind how a car actually behaves on track, in a way that's accessible to other students and engineers.
You'll find this content also on Instagram, where I post shorter breakdowns and visualizations alongside the code here.

### What's inside:

- Tire modeling and force-slip relationships
- Suspension kinematics and geometry analysis
- Load transfer and weight distribution calculations
- Lap time simulation tools
- Notes and derivations connecting the math to real-world vehicle behavior

## Why:
Formula Student gave me a hands-on foundation in vehicle dynamics and made me interested into searching and studying more around this topic in specifi — this is where I document, formalize, and build on that knowledge with the long-term goal of working in professional motorsport.
